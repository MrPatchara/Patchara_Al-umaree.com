Nokov Motion Capture Training

Optical Mocap for Sports, VR, and Real-time Animation

Optical Motion Capture Biomechanics Unreal/VR Streaming

Intensive hands-on training at Nokov’s Beijing HQ focused on optical motion capture for sports science, biomechanics, and interactive applications. Covered end-to-end mocap workflows—from marker setup and data cleaning to exporting and streaming for real-time experiences.

Key Takeaways

Sports Analysis

Applied mocap to assess movement mechanics, reduce injury risk, and optimize training plans.

Markersets & Fixes

Built Helen Hayes markerset; repaired lost markers with cubic joint interpolation for clean datasets.

Data Export

Exported C3D/BVH for downstream biomechanics analysis and animation pipelines.

Real-time Streaming

Streamed mocap into Unreal Engine and VR gaming workflows for live animation and immersive demos.

Training Coverage

  • Introduction to Nokov optical mocap for sports analysis
  • Creating and managing markersets (Helen Hayes and custom)
  • Repairing lost markers via cubic joint interpolation
  • Exporting mocap data (C3D, BVH) for analysis and animation
  • Implementing mocap for VR gaming scenarios
  • Streaming mocap to Unreal Engine for real-time animation
“Motion capture is a powerful tool to refine technique, minimize injury risk, and elevate both sports performance and interactive experiences.”
